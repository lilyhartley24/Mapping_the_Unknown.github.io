UFO Word Cloud: Descriptions of Sightings

Data Source

This visualization uses UFO sighting reports from the National UFO Reporting Center (NUFORC). The dataset includes approximately 70,000 records with attributes such as datetime, location, shape, and user-submitted comments describing each sighting.

Processing

The word cloud is generated from cleaned comment text, filtered to U.S. sightings from 2010 onward with durations longer than two minutes. Common noise such as URLs was removed before generating the visualization.

Key Takeaways

This word cloud highlights the most frequently used terms in UFO sighting descriptions, revealing how individuals commonly interpret and describe unexplained aerial phenomena. Words such as “light,” “object,” “orange,” and “flying” dominate the visualization, suggesting that sightings are often described in terms of brightness, motion, and color rather than clearly defined structures. The presence of geometric terms like “triangle,” “circle,” and “orb” indicates recurring visual patterns in reported sightings. Overall, this visualization suggests that while UFO experiences vary, people tend to rely on a consistent set of descriptive language when reporting what they observe, reflecting shared perception patterns and the ambiguity of these events.
