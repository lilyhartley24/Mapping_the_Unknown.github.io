Interactive Map: Geographic Distribution of UFO Sightings

Data source
This visualization uses scrubbed.csv from the National UFO Reporting Center (NUFORC), including geographic coordinates for reported UFO sightings across the United States.
How the data was prepared
The map uses latitude and longitude data from individual sighting reports to display where UFO sightings are most concentrated. By plotting reports spatially, the visualization helps reveal regional clusters and broader geographic patterns.
Interaction
Use the Tableau map controls to zoom, pan, and inspect areas of higher and lower sighting concentration across the country.

Takeaway

This visualization shows that UFO sightings are not evenly distributed across the United States. Instead, reports tend to cluster in specific regions, particularly along the coasts and in more densely populated areas. These spatial patterns suggest that UFO reporting may be influenced not only by where unusual aerial phenomena are observed, but also by population density, visibility conditions, and the likelihood that people in certain regions are more able or willing to report what they see. Overall, the map highlights strong geographic concentration rather than a uniform national pattern.
